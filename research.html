Photochromic windows

Review: The future of smart fenestration?

Photochromic materials available today are either not durable enough for use in smart windows (as with organic dyes) or cannot be fabricated using scalable methods (as with silver halides). For this reason, the fabrication of photochromic smart windows remains out of reach. Could an emerging family of photochromic materials, oxyhydrides, change this scenario?

Explore the full discussion in my SOLMAT 2024 review.

Magnetic Properties of GdHO

Gadolinium oxyhydride (GdHO) is a photochromic material that darkens under light and reverts by thermal relaxation. Easily deposited by magnetron sputtering, GdHO shows promise for smart glazing applications. Our study explores GdHO’s photochromic mechanism, highlighting the role of its paramagnetic properties. The reversible increase in Curie–Weiss temperature under illumination suggests RKKY interactions, consistent with reduced resistivity in darkened films.

Read more in my SSC 2024 paper.
