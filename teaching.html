Project Jupyter4UU

The project Jupyter4UU arose from the idea that Uppsala University could benefit from further integrating Jupyter notebooks into its curriculum and research practices, aligning more closely with the advancements seen globally at other top universities in the world.
On this basis, during the year 2023 I coordinated the Jupyter4UU project, whose objective was to change this scenario at Uppsala University. ... Recognizing the potential of Jupyter notebooks to revolutionize teaching and learning, I initiated this project to promote their widespread adoption within our educational community. Through a series of targeted workshops, hands-on training sessions, and the development of accessible resources, my aim has been to equip educators with the skills and knowledge needed to integrate Jupyter notebooks into their courses.
Furthermore, Jupyter4UU aimed to promote the use of open-source tools, such as Jupyter and Python, over proprietary tools like MATLAB, aligning with both worldwide trends and Uppsala University's policies. This shift is not solely about eliminating paywalls, ensuring that all students and educators, regardless of their economic background, have equal access to the necessary technological resources. It is also about the substantial advantages in terms of accessibility, openness, and reproducibility of results.
Indeed, the transparency inherent in open-source tools enables an environment of openness, which is critical for scientific research and education. Jupyter notebooks facilitate collaborative efforts across different disciplines and institutions and are, therefore, crucial skills for the future professional development of our educators and students.
By adopting open-source tools, we also become part of a community that values transparency and shared information. This community-driven approach encourages continuous improvement and innovation, as users can contribute to its development.
